Company Overview

Alphabet Inc. (GOOGL) is a global technology conglomerate operating primarily through its Google segment, which includes search, advertising, cloud computing, hardware, and digital services. Revenue has grown from $257.6 billion in 2021 to $350.0 billion in 2024, representing a compound annual growth rate of 11.8%. Revenue is projected to reach $466.4 billion by 2027.

Financial Performance and Profitability Trends

EBITDA expanded from $103.5 billion in 2021 to $135.4 billion in 2024, with the EBITDA margin improving from 40.2% to 38.7% over the same period. The company experienced a margin contraction in 2022, when EBITDA margin declined to 30.1%. The subsequent recovery brought the 2024 EBITDA margin close to 2021 levels. Projections indicate continued margin expansion to 51.7% by 2027, reflecting operational leverage. The contribution margin has strengthened from 56.9% in 2021 to 58.2% in 2024, with forecasts indicating further improvement to 62.7% by 2027.

Operational Efficiency and Cost Structure

SG&A expenses as a percentage of revenue declined from 15.0% in 2022 to 12.0% in 2024, with projections indicating further compression to 11.0% by 2027. This trend indicates improving operational scalability as the company grows. Cost of operations increased from $110.9 billion in 2021 to $146.3 billion in 2024, growing at a slower pace than revenue and enabling the expansion in contribution margins.

Earnings and Valuation Perspective

Earnings per share has grown from $5.69 in 2021 to $8.13 in 2024, and is projected to reach $13.01 by 2027. This represents significant EPS growth driven by both revenue expansion and margin improvement. The PE ratio has fluctuated between approximately 19x and 29x over the historical period, with the 2024 multiple of 23.3x and forward estimates suggesting a decline to 24.6x by 2027.
