NVIDIA Corporation

# NVIDIA Corporation (NVDA) ## AI Infrastructure Leader Delivering Exceptional Growth Amid Market Leadership in Accelerated Computing **Investment Thesis:** NVIDIA has transformed from a graphics chip manufacturer into a leading provider of AI infrastructure, capturing substantial demand for data center GPUs. The company's revenue trajectory—from $27B in 2022A to a projected $250B by 2027E (68.3% CAGR)—reflects its significant role in generative AI adoption and accelerated computing deployment across cloud hyperscalers, enterprises, and sovereign AI initiatives. **Financial Performance Highlights:** - **Revenue Growth Trajectory:** Revenue increased 126% in 2024A to $61B, followed by 114% growth in 2025A to $130B. Projections indicate continued expansion at 65.5% in 2026A, moderating to 4-6% annually through 2027E as the revenue base scales. These forward estimates (2025E-2027E) are based on analyst consensus and assume sustained data center demand. - **Margin Expansion:** EBITDA margins expanded from 22% in 2023A to 66-67% in 2024A-2026A, with projections reaching 73.5% by 2027E. This improvement reflects a shift in product mix toward higher-margin data center GPUs (which command premium pricing relative to gaming products) and operating leverage as fixed costs are absorbed across a larger revenue base. - **Operating Efficiency:** Contribution margins have stabilized in the 71-75% range (2024A-2027E) despite ongoing supply chain investments. SG&A as a percentage of revenue improved from 9.0% (2023A) to 2.1% (2026A), with projections declining to 0.6% by 2027E. This projected decline assumes significant revenue scaling with controlled absolute SG&A growth, though such low levels would be exceptional for a company of this scale and warrant monitoring. - **Earnings Growth:** EPS increased from $0.18 (2023A) to $4.93 (2026A), with projections reaching $5.88 by 2027E, driven by both revenue growth and margin expansion. **Valuation Context:** The stock trades at 35.9x forward P/E (2025E), down from 62.7x in 2022A. This multiple compression reflects earnings scaling faster than share price appreciation as the company transitions from high-growth to very-large-scale operations. The current valuation represents a premium to broader technology indices, reflecting NVIDIA's market position, though investors should assess this against semiconductor and cloud infrastructure peers. **Key Considerations:** Critical factors for the investment case include successful execution on next-generation architectures (Blackwell and beyond), supply chain capacity expansion to meet demand, competitive positioning against emerging alternatives from AMD, custom silicon efforts by hyperscalers, and the sustainability of elevated data center capital expenditure cycles. The projected SG&A efficiency gains and sustained high margins assume continued market leadership and may face pressure from increased competition or market maturation.
