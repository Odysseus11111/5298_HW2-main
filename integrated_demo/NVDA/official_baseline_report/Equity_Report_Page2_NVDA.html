COMPETITOR ANALYSIS

### Competitor Analysis NVIDIA Corporation (NVDA) operates in a highly competitive landscape, primarily against Advanced Micro Devices (AMD) and Intel Corporation (INTC). The following analysis highlights key financial metrics and performance indicators for NVDA relative to its peers. #### Financial Performance - **Revenue Growth**: NVIDIA's revenue is projected to grow significantly from $26.9 billion in 2022 to approximately $250 billion by 2027, reflecting a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 68.3%. In contrast, AMD and INTC have shown varying revenue trajectories, with AMD's revenue expected to reach $8.6 billion by 2027 and INTC's projected at $47.4 billion. - **EBITDA**: NVIDIA's EBITDA is anticipated to increase from $11.4 billion in 2022 to approximately $183.7 billion by 2027. This growth positions NVDA favorably compared to AMD and INTC, which are projected to have EBITDA of $8.6 billion and $47.4 billion, respectively, in 2027. - **Contribution Margin**: NVIDIA's contribution margin is expected to improve from 64.9% in 2022 to 74.1% in 2027, indicating enhanced operational efficiency. This is significantly higher than AMD and INTC, which have lower margins. - **EBITDA Margin**: The EBITDA margin for NVIDIA is projected to rise from 42.2% in 2022 to 73.5% in 2027, showcasing strong profitability compared to its peers. AMD and INTC are expected to have EBITDA margins of 38.3% and 106.9% in 2024, respectively, but these figures are not directly comparable due to the different growth trajectories. #### Valuation Metrics - **Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio**: As of 2022, NVIDIA's P/E ratio stood at approximately 62.7, which is higher than AMD's and INTC's ratios, indicating a premium valuation likely due to its growth prospects. By 2027, NVDA's P/E ratio is projected to decrease to 32.4, suggesting a potential normalization as earnings grow. - **EV/EBITDA**: In 2022, NVIDIA's EV/EBITDA ratio was approximately 54.7, significantly higher than AMD's 17.9 and INTC's 6.5. This reflects market expectations of future growth, although it also indicates that NVIDIA is currently valued at a premium compared to its competitors. #### Conclusion NVIDIA is positioned as a leader in the semiconductor industry, with robust growth prospects and superior profitability metrics compared to AMD and INTC. The company's ability to maintain high margins and significant revenue growth will be critical in sustaining its competitive advantage. Investors should consider these factors when evaluating NVIDIA's market position and future performance relative to its peers.
