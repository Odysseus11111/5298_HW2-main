COMPETITOR ANALYSIS

### Competitor Analysis Apple Inc. (AAPL) operates in a highly competitive landscape, primarily against major technology firms such as Alphabet Inc. (GOOGL) and Microsoft Corp. (MSFT). The following analysis compares key financial metrics and performance indicators among these competitors. #### Revenue and Growth For the fiscal year 2021, Apple reported revenue of approximately $365.8 billion, with projected revenue growth to around $481.7 billion by 2027, reflecting a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 3.3%. In comparison, GOOGL and MSFT's revenue figures for 2021 are not available in this report, but their growth trajectories will be critical in assessing competitive positioning. #### Profitability Metrics Apple's contribution profit for 2021 was approximately $152.8 billion, with a contribution margin of 41.8%. The EBITDA for the same year was reported at $123.1 billion, yielding an EBITDA margin of 33.7%. These metrics indicate strong operational efficiency relative to its peers. In terms of EBITDA, Apple has consistently performed well, with figures of $123.1 billion in 2021, $133.1 billion in 2022, and a slight decline to $129.2 billion in 2023. GOOGL and MSFT reported EBITDA figures of $103.5 billion and $85.1 billion in 2021, respectively, indicating that Apple has maintained a competitive edge in profitability. #### Valuation Metrics The price-to-earnings (P/E) ratio for Apple was approximately 25.9 in 2021, with an expected increase to 29.2 by 2027. This valuation suggests that investors are willing to pay a premium for Apple's earnings relative to its peers. For context, GOOGL and MSFT's P/E ratios are not provided in this report, but they will be essential for a comprehensive valuation comparison. #### Conclusion Apple's financial metrics indicate a robust competitive position within the technology sector, characterized by strong revenue generation, profitability, and a favorable valuation. Continuous monitoring of GOOGL and MSFT's performance will be necessary to assess shifts in competitive dynamics and market share.

### Major Takeaways Apple Inc. (AAPL) is projected to experience steady revenue growth, with a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 3.3% from 2021 to 2027, increasing from $365.8 billion in 2021 to an estimated $481.7 billion in 2027. EBITDA is forecasted to grow from $123.1 billion in 2021 to approximately $215.8 billion in 2027, with the EBITDA margin also expected to increase from 33.7% to 44.8%. In comparison to peers, Apple’s EBITDA for 2023 is estimated at $129.2 billion, positioning it favorably against Alphabet (GOOGL) and Microsoft (MSFT). The enterprise value to EBITDA (EV/EBITDA) ratio for Apple is projected to be 21.6 in 2023, which is competitive within the industry. Overall, Apple Inc. is positioned for continued growth with improving margins and profitability metrics, making it a strong candidate for investment consideration.
