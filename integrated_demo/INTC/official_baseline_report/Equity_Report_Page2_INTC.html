COMPETITOR ANALYSIS

### Competitor Analysis Intel Corporation (INTC) operates in a highly competitive semiconductor industry, facing significant competition from peers such as Advanced Micro Devices (AMD) and NVIDIA (NVDA). The following analysis provides insights into Intel's financial performance relative to its competitors based on available data. #### Financial Performance **Revenue and Growth**: In 2021, Intel reported revenue of approximately $79.02 billion, which is projected to decline to about $61.18 billion by 2027, reflecting a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of -9.6%. In contrast, AMD's revenue has shown a more favorable trajectory, with significant growth expected in the coming years. **EBITDA Comparison**: Intel's EBITDA for 2021 was approximately $33.87 billion, which is significantly higher than AMD's EBITDA of $4.17 billion for the same year. However, Intel's EBITDA is projected to decrease sharply, with estimates of only $18.72 billion by 2027. AMD's EBITDA is expected to grow to approximately $8.60 billion in 2027, indicating a more robust growth outlook. **Margins**: Intel's contribution margin was 55.4% in 2021, which is higher than AMD's and indicates strong profitability. However, this margin is expected to decline to 37.8% by 2027. The EBITDA margin for Intel was 42.9% in 2021, projected to decrease to 30.6% by 2027, suggesting increasing cost pressures compared to its peers. **Earnings Per Share (EPS)**: Intel's EPS was $4.89 in 2021 but is projected to turn negative by 2027, with an estimate of -$0.07. This contrasts sharply with AMD, which is expected to show positive growth in EPS over the same period. #### Valuation Metrics **Price-to-Earnings (PE) Ratio**: Intel's PE ratio was approximately 10.48 in 2021, but it is projected to become negative by 2027, indicating a potential loss of investor confidence as earnings decline. In comparison, AMD's PE ratio has been more favorable, reflecting better market sentiment. **EV/EBITDA**: In 2021, Intel's EV/EBITDA was approximately 7.13, which is lower than AMD's 42.10, indicating a more attractive valuation relative to its earnings before interest, taxes, depreciation, and amortization. However, this metric is expected to fluctuate significantly in the coming years, particularly as Intel's EBITDA declines. #### Conclusion Intel faces substantial challenges in maintaining its competitive position in the semiconductor market. The projected decline in revenue, EBITDA, and EPS, coupled with decreasing margins, highlights the need for strategic initiatives to enhance growth and profitability. In contrast, AMD and NVIDIA appear to be on more favorable growth trajectories, which may impact Intel's market share and investor sentiment moving forward.
